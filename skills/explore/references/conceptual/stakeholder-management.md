# Stakeholder Management

## Key Concepts

Stakeholder influence is iterative intelligence, not a single pitch. Each interaction is a data-gathering opportunity — understand their world before trying to change their mind. Map power dynamics, decision styles, and hidden motivations. The goal isn't to win an argument; it's to make your proposal feel like their idea.

## Framework Questions

- What does this stakeholder need to achieve?
- What's at risk for them if things change?
- What do they care about — speed, control, recognition, data?
- How do they make decisions — alone, by committee, evidence-driven?

## Questions

- Where does this stakeholder sit in terms of power and interest — can they block you, and do they care enough to try?
- What does success look like *from their perspective* — not yours?
- What threatens their status, certainty, autonomy, or sense of fairness?
- Who influences *them* — whose opinion do they trust?
- What's their communication style — do they want the bottom line or the full analysis?
- What have you tried before with this person, and what happened?
- What's the one thing you don't know about them that would change your approach?
