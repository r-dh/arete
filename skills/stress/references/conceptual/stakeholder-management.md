# Stakeholder Management

## Key Concepts

Conversation preparation is tactical influence. Before any stakeholder interaction, rehearse the specific ask, anticipate objections, and prepare questions that let them articulate the problem themselves. The best influence happens when you stop trying to convince and start helping them think through constraints.

## Refinement Questions

- What's the single thing you need from this stakeholder after the next interaction?
- What's the worst thing they could say, and how would you respond?
- What situation question would establish shared context without putting them on the defensive?
- What problem question would help *them* articulate the pain — not you telling them?
- What implication question would make the cost of inaction real for *them*?
- What need-payoff question would let *them* describe the benefit of your proposal?
- What are the top three negative things they're probably thinking about your proposal? Say them out loud.
- How would you label their emotion? "It seems like you're concerned about..."
- What "how" or "what" question could you ask that forces them to think through your constraints?
- Are you going in to convince, or to learn? If convince — you're not ready.
- What's the one question you're afraid to ask them? That's probably the one you need to ask.
